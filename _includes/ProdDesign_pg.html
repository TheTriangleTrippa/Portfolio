Product Design 1st Year

    Design and develop a childrens toy

    The first assignment completed was a design for a childrens’ toy, while I stretched the assignment description allowing for my childish instincts to lead me towards playing with a a sword in “Battle Royal” - an adventure based duelling game over controlling digital environments like a real life video game.

    The assignment was to conceptualise a target market, investigate some avenues to present a toy for children through the design process and prototyping to produce an innovative toy that meet the given assignment specifications.

    My final design fit a duelling sword, multiple ’bases’ where the players would control the domain maintaining control to win the round. The swords interact with the players on touch, through induction, the swords generate voltage spikes that would register a hit on the opponents.

Product Design 2nd Year

    Material investigation and materials in design.

    I was assigned PLA to research and design with, this involved working to manufacture a prototype, working from recycled material sources.The intent was that each group was given a different recycled material, and we were to utilise the specific material properties to produce a suitable product from our allocated material.

    After much research, and many other concepts we came to a pen. Because of how many pens are produced in the world, and the lack of recyclability of a pen - we decided that the biodegradability of PLA was perfect for the pen that was inevitably intended for the landfill.

    This assignment was also to test our understanding of manufacturing processes intended for plastics - having us work with plastics understanding the properties and complications of product manufacturing. Ideally the result of our design product given proper manufacturing facilities would allow us to understand and produce our finland design considering the material implications. This was another group assignment, presenting a model, promotional video and design package.

    Playground equipment design.

    Designed a piece of playground equipment. Initially I had to design an existing piece of equipment - this was more of a CAD exercise - inclusive of aspects of mechanical, manufacturing and functional nature relevant to the equipment.

    These details can be seen in the CAD model of the flying below.

    I was then required to design an entire piece using the full design process, this was a group activity, presenting a full portfolio on completion.

    Our final design fit local legislation for playgrounds regarding safety, constructed from materials that were specifically chosen to be durable given the environmental conditions and were given their intended use.     Waterclock - mark the passage of time with only a waterpump

    A water-clock utilising thermofluid dynamics was designed and manufactured to mark a 5 minute interval from a water source with a pump. In this assignment we were not allowed to use 3D-printed materials and were awarded points for style and functionality.

    My design utilised a simple ratchet mechanism that was used to control the rotation of a water feed wheel, this in turn allowed for a tick at each ratchet of this wheel driving a clock face by a belt. There are significantly more features in this design than first appears, and was completed through various fluid dynamic principles being calculated to achieve the required performance. This clock achieved 5 minutes with wheel and rachet correctly aligned.

Product Design 3rd Year

    Wall bike mount via various design and manufacturing techniques.

    The first third year project was to design a bike wall mount using flatpack fastenerless construction, this was designed to hold the weight of a bicycle and was tested at the end of the assignment to specified Factor of Safety.

    The second part of the assignment was to use FEA and Generative Design to develop this design to hold within a specified weight limit at a defined FoS. In my instance I used generative design, alongside FEA to determine mounting points and critical regions that would allow for the generated bike mount to meet this defined prerequisite. The design was modelled with consideration of a real bike with weights to scale for testing.

    In this assignment, I changed groups between phases 1 and 2 working from a different base point. My apologies - I have yet to pull out images of the first phase from my work.
